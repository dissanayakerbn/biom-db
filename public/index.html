What is CKDu?

Chronic kidney disease of unknown etiology (CKDu) is the gradual loss of kidney function over periods of months or years with symptoms appearing in the late stages of the disease. Emergence of CKDu is a global health concern with more than 10% of the worldwide population being affected by it. It has become increasingly prevalent in agricultural communities in developing countries including Sri Lanka, India and Latin. Although the prevalence of CKDu has progressively become more widespread, the underlying molecular mechanisms of the disease remain largely unknown.

What is BIOM?

BIOM is a data base that allows you to view most significant details of CKDu related specific biomarkers. You can select preloaded details of existing and novel CKDu related biomarkers through selecting relevant tab. The data base itself is unique due to these novels CKDu related biomarkers are identified by our research team. Further, we are providing access to interesting researchers to upload your details of CKDu related biomarkers through “Upload your CKDu related Biomarker”
